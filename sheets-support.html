Getting started

  1. Open your spreadsheet and go to Extensions → LinkSheet → Open.
  2. In the sidebar, choose the Google Drive™ folder that holds your files.
  3. (Optional) Turn on Partial match if your filenames don't exactly match your cell values.
  4. Pick which tabs to link — the active sheet, selected tabs, or all of them.
  5. Click Create Hyperlinks and review the results report.

Frequently asked questions

I don't see LinkSheet in the Extensions menu.

After installing, reload the spreadsheet tab. The add-on appears under Extensions → LinkSheet. If it's still missing, confirm the add-on is installed for the Google account you're currently signed in to.

It says "Google hasn't verified this app."

While our verification with Google is in progress, you may see an "unverified app" screen on first use. This is normal for newer add-ons. You can proceed by choosing Advanced → Go to LinkSheet, or wait until verification completes.

No links were created / some cells didn't match.

LinkSheet matches a cell's value to a file name (case-insensitive, ignoring the file extension). If a cell didn't link, the value likely doesn't match a filename in the chosen folder. Try turning on Partial match, and check the results report — it lists ambiguous matches and duplicate filenames for you to resolve.

Will it change or delete my data?

No. LinkSheet only adds hyperlinks to matching cells in your spreadsheet. It skips cells that already have a link, never overwrites formulas or pivot-table output, and never modifies your Google Drive™. To undo a run, use File → Version history in Google Sheets™, or use the add-on's Clear Hyperlinks feature.

What does LinkSheet access?

Read-only, metadata-only Drive access (file names and links only — never file contents) and the active spreadsheet. Full detail is in our Privacy Policy.

How do I remove the add-on?

Go to Extensions → Add-ons → Manage add-ons, find LinkSheet, and choose Uninstall.
